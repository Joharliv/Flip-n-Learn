Profile

Definition

  • Cell biology is the study of cells, which are the basic structural and functional units of life. It covers cell structure, function, and processes, providing insights into how organisms grow, develop, and function.

Types of cells

  • Prokaryotic Cells: These are simple, single-celled organisms like bacteria. They lack a nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles, with DNA floating freely within the cell.

  • Eukaryotic Cells: Found in plants, animals, fungi, and protists, these complex cells contain a nucleus and various organelles that perform specific functions.

Cell Structures

  • Cell Membrane:The cell membrane is a protective barrier that regulates what enters and exits the cell. It is semi-permeable and composed of a lipid bilayer with embedded proteins.

  • Nucleus: Often referred to as the "control center" of the cell, the nucleus contains DNA and is responsible for regulating cellular functions and gene expression.

  • Mitochondria:Known as the "powerhouse" of the cell, mitochondria produce energy (ATP) through cellular respiration.

  • Ribosomes:These tiny structures are sites of protein synthesis, where amino acids are assembled into proteins.

Functions of Cells

  • Cells perform various essential functions such as energy production, waste removal, protein synthesis.

  • They are also responsible for growth, repair, and reproduction in multicellular organisms.

  • Cells communicate with each other and respond to signals in their environment
