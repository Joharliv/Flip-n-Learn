Profile

International Economics

  • International Economics studies the flow of goods, services, and capital across countries, focusing on trade policies, exchange rates, and the impact of globalization on national economies.

Tariffs

  • Tariffs are taxes imposed on imported goods, making them more expensive. Tariffs aim to protect domestic industries from foreign competition but can lead to higher prices for consumers.

Exchange Rates

  • Exchange Rates are the prices at which one currency can be exchanged for another. They fluctuate based on factors like interest rates, inflation, and economic stability, affecting international trade and investment.

World Trade Organization

  • The WTO regulates international trade by creating rules, resolving disputes, and promoting fair trade practices. Its goal is to encourage free trade while considering the needs of developing countries.

Balance of Payments

  • The Balance of Payments is a record of all economic transactions between a country and the rest of the world, including trade, investment, and financial transfers. It consists of the current account, capital account, and financial account.
