What is Thermodynamics?

  • Thermodynamics is the study of energy changes, especially heat and work, during chemical and physical processes. It helps explain how energy flows and transforms.

System and Surroundings

  • System:

    The part of the universe under study (e.g., a reaction in a beaker).

  • Surroundings:

    Everything outside the system that can exchange energy with it.

Types of Systems

  • Open System:

    Exchanges both matter and energy with surroundings.

  • Closed System:

    Exchanges only energy, not matter.

  • Isolated System:

    Does not exchange matter or energy.

First Law of Thermodynamics

  • FLOT states that Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only change forms.

  • Mathematically, ΔU = q + w, where ΔU is the change in internal energy, q is heat, and w is work.

Second Law of Thermodynamics

  • The Second Law states that entropy (disorder) of an isolated system always increases over time. For spontaneous processes, total entropy of the system and surroundings increases.

Standard Enthalpy of Formation

  • The enthalpy change when 1 mole of a compound forms from its elements in their standard states. Denoted as ΔHf°, it's a benchmark for calculating reaction enthalpies.

Gibbs Free Energy (G)

  • Gibbs free energy (G) is a thermodynamic potential that predicts reaction spontaneity at constant temperature and pressure. ΔG = ΔH - TΔS. If ΔG < 0, the process is spontaneous.
