Financial Economics

  • Financial Economics studies how resources are allocated in markets over time, focusing on the pricing of assets, the role of financial institutions, and the impact of risk and uncertainty on financial decisions.

Risk and Return

  • Risk refers to the potential variability in returns from an investment. Return is the profit made from an investment. Generally, higher risk is associated with the potential for higher returns and vice versa.

Present Value

  • Present Value is the current worth of a future sum of money, discounted at a specific interest rate. It helps determine the value today of cash flows expected in the future.

Efficient Market Hypothesis

  • The Efficient Market Hypothesis suggests that all known information is already reflected in asset prices, implying it is impossible to consistently achieve higher-than-average returns through stock-picking or market timing.

Capital Asset Pricing Model

  • CAPM is a model used to determine the expected return on an investment based on its risk, as measured by beta, and the expected market return. It helps investors assess if an asset offers a reasonable return given its risk level.
