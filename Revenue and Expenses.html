Profile

Revenue

  • Revenue is the income generated from normal business operations, typically from the sale of goods or services to customers.

Gross Profit

  • Gross Profit is calculated as Revenue minus the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). It indicates the profitability of core business activities before operating expenses.

Cost of Goods Sold

  • Cost of Goods Sold represents the direct costs of producing goods or services sold by a company, including raw materials and direct labor, but excluding indirect costs.

Fixed and Variable Expenses

  • Fixed expenses remain constant regardless of production levels (e.g., rent),

  • Variable expenses fluctuate with production or sales volume (e.g., materials, commissions).

Matching Principle

  • The Matching Principle states that expenses should be recorded in the same period as the revenues they help generate, providing an accurate representation of profit.
