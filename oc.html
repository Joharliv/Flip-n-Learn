Profile

Hydrocarbons

  • Hydrocarbons are organic compounds composed entirely of hydrogen and carbon. They are divided into alkanes (single bonds), alkenes (double bonds), and alkynes (triple bonds).

Functional Groups

  • Functional groups are specific groups of atoms within molecules that determine the chemical properties of organic compounds, such as -OH (alcohols), -COOH (carboxylic acids), and -NH₂ (amines).

Isomerism

  • Isomerism includes structural isomerism (different bonding patterns) and stereoisomerism (same bonding pattern, different spatial arrangement), such as geometric and optical isomers.

Alkyl Halides (Haloalkanes)

  • Alkyl halides (haloalkanes) are compounds where one or more hydrogen atoms in an alkane are replaced by halogens (F, Cl, Br, or I).

Aromatic Compounds

  • Aromatic compounds contain one or more benzene-like rings, which exhibit stability due to delocalized pi electrons. They undergo electrophilic substitution reactions, like nitration and halogenation.

Types of Reactions in Organic Chemistry

  • Addition Reactions:

    Common in alkenes/alkynes (e.g., hydrogenation).

  • Substitution Reactions:

    Common in saturated compounds and aromatics.

  • Elimination Reactions:

    Removes atoms/groups to form double or triple bonds.

  • Oxidation-Reduction Reactions:

    Changes the oxidation state of carbon atoms..
