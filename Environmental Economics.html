Environmental Economics

  • Environmental Economics is a branch of economics focused on how economic activities affect the environment and how policies can address issues such as pollution, resource depletion, and climate change.

Tragedy of the Commons

  • The Tragedy of the Commons describes a situation where individuals, acting in their own interest, deplete shared resources, resulting in resource degradation for everyone.

  • Examples include overfishing and deforestation.

Carbon Pricing

  • Carbon Pricing is an approach to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by assigning a cost to carbon emissions, either through a carbon tax or a cap-and-trade system, encouraging companies to reduce their carbon footprint.

Cap-and-Trade System

  • A Cap-and-Trade System sets a limit on total emissions and allows companies to buy and sell emission permits. This creates a financial incentive to reduce emissions as firms with lower emissions can sell their unused permits.

Pigouvian Tax

  • A Pigouvian Tax is a tax imposed on activities that create negative externalities, such as pollution, to reflect the social cost of the activity and incentivize reducing the harmful effects.
