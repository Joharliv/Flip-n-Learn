Basic Probability Definition

  • Probability of an event E is given by:
  • No. of favourable outcomes/ Total no. of outcomes
  • Range: 0≤P(E)≤1

Types of Events

  • Simple Event:

    One outcome, e.g., rolling a die shows a 3.

  • Compound Event:

    More than one outcome, e.g., rolling a sum of 7.

  • Mutually Exclusive Events:

    Cannot occur simultaneously, e.g., flipping heads or tails.

  • Non-Mutually Exclusive Events:

    Can occur together, e.g., drawing a red card from a deck.

Addition Rule

  • Mutually Exclusive:

    P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)

  • Non-Mutually Exclusive:

    P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Multiplication Rule

  • Independent Events:

    P(A ∩ B) = P(A) . P(B)

  • Dependent Events:

    P(A ∩ B) = P(A) . P(B|A)

Conditional Probability

  • P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B)/P(B)

  • It measures the probability of event A occurring under the condition that B has occurred.

Bayes' Theorem

  • P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B).P(A)/P(B)

  • Bayes' Theorem calculates the probability of an event based on prior knowledge and new evidence.
