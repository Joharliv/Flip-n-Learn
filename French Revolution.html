Profile

French Revolution

  • The French Revolution was a significant social and political upheaval in France from 1789 to 1799 that fundamentally changed French society and influenced global history.

Causes of French Revolution

  • Social Inequality:

    The division of society into three estates (clergy, nobility, commoners) created severe social tensions, as the commoners bore the tax burden.

  • Economic Hardships:

    France faced financial crisis from wars, including support for the American Revolution, and high taxes.

  • Enlightenment Ideas:

    New ideas about equality, liberty, and democracy spread, inspiring demands for political reform.

  • Weak Leadership:

    King Louis XVI's indecisive and extravagant rule added to the unrest.

Storming of the Bastille

  • Event:

    Parisians stormed the Bastille prison, a symbol of royal tyranny, searching for weapons and ammunition.

  • Importance:

    This event symbolized the people’s revolt against oppression and marked a significant victory for revolutionaries.

  • Result:

    Bastille Day is now celebrated as France's national day, commemorating the beginning of the Revolution.

Reign of Terror (1793-1794)

  • Period:

    Led by Maximilien Robespierre and the Committee of Public Safety to protect the Revolution from perceived enemies.

  • Events:

    Thousands of people were executed by guillotine, including King Louis XVI and Queen Marie Antoinette

  • Outcome:

    The extreme measures led to fear and chaos, ultimately ending with Robespierre’s execution, signaling the end of the Reign of Terror.

Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte

  • Emergence:

    Amidst the chaos following the Revolution, Napoleon, a successful military general, gained popularity.

  • 1799 Coup:

    He staged a coup d'état, overthrowing the government (Directory) and establishing himself as First Consul.

  • Significane:

    Napoleon ended the Revolution and began a new era in French history, eventually declaring himself Emperor and expanding French influence across Europe.
