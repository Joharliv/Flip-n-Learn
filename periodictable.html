Profile

Periodic Table Structure

  • The periodic table is arranged by atomic number with 7 periods (horizontal rows) and 18 groups (vertical columns). Each group shares chemical properties due to similar valence electrons.

Periodic Trends: Atomic Radius

  • Decreases across a period (left to right) due to increased nuclear charge.

  • Increases down a group as more electron shells are added.

Alkali and Alkaline Earth Metals

  • Alkali Metals (Group 1):

    Highly reactive, soft metals, one valence electron, e.g., sodium.

  • Alkaline Earth Metals (Group 2):

    Less reactive than alkali metals, two valence electrons, e.g., magnesium.

Halogens and Noble Gases

  • Halogens (Group 17):

    Very reactive, used in disinfection and lighting, e.g., chlorine.

  • Noble Gases (Group 18):

    Inert, stable gases due to full valence shells, e.g., neon.

Lanthanides and Actinides

  • Lanthanides and actinides are f-block elements found below the main table. Lanthanides are used in electronics, while actinides, like uranium, are often radioactive.

Electronegativity

  • Electronegativity is an atom’s ability to attract electrons in a bond. It increases across a period and decreases down a group.
