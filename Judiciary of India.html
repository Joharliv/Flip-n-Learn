Profile

Judiciary of India

  • The Judiciary of India is an independent branch of government that interprets and upholds the Constitution, ensuring justice and protecting citizens' rights.

Supreme Court of India

  • Establishment:

    Established in 1950 as the highest court under India’s Constitution.

  • Composition:

    Consists of the Chief Justice of India (CJI) and up to 33 other judges.

  • Significance:

    Its decisions are binding on all lower courts, and it serves as the ultimate interpreter of the Constitution.

High Courts

  • Jurisdiction:

    Have original jurisdiction in certain cases (e.g., election disputes, writ petitions) and appellate jurisdiction over civil and criminal cases from subordinate courts.

  • Composition:

    Each High Court is headed by a Chief Justice, with additional judges as required.

  • Significance:

    Protects the rights of citizens at the state level and ensures the application of the law within the state.

Subordinate Courts

  • Levels:

    Includes District Courts, Magistrate Courts, and other courts handling specific matters.

  • Jurisdiction:

    District Courts have jurisdiction over civil and criminal cases in each district, with cases divided based on seriousness.

  • Significance:

    Forms the base of the judiciary, handling the bulk of cases and directly impacting citizens’ day-to-day legal matters.

Independence of Judiciary

  • Principle:

    The judiciary operates independently from the executive and legislative branches.

  • Safeguards:

    Judges are appointed through a specific process, have security of tenure, and are protected from arbitrary removal to maintain impartiality.

  • Importance:

    Ensures fair and unbiased rulings, and allows the judiciary to act as a protector of citizens' rights and an interpreter of the Constitution.
