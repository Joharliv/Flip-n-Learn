What is a coordination compound?

  • A coordination compound consists of a central metal atom or ion surrounded by a set of ligands that are bonded to it.

What are ligands?

  • Ligands are ions or molecules that donate a pair of electrons to the central metal atom/ion to form a coordination bond.

Types of Ligands

  • Monodentate Ligand:

    Form only one bond with a metal ion, having a single donor atom.

  • Bidentate Ligand:

    Have two donor atoms capable of forming two bonds with a metal ion.

  • Polydentate Ligand:

    Occupy many sites of the same metal ion.

  • Ambidentate Ligand:

    Can bond through different atoms.

What is a coordination number?

  • The coordination number is the number of ligand atoms that are directly bonded to the central metal atom/ion in a coordination compound.

Crystal Field Theory

  • Crystal field theory explains the splitting of d-orbital energies of metal ions in coordination compounds due to the presence of ligands.

What is a complex ion?

  • A complex ion is a charged species consisting of a central metal atom/ion bonded to one or more ligands.
